Facts About Waterford

Just because we love Waterford so much, here are 20 funny and interesting facts about County Waterford.

  1. Waterford has many names (and here are 3 of them):
    • Port Lairge: The Irish name for Waterford. The translation of Port Lairge means ‘leg shaped river’, from the air it is obvious where this name came from.
    • Cuan-na-Grian: The ancient Celtic name for Waterford was “Cuan na Graí” or “The Harbour of the Sun”. If you’ve ever travelled up the river at dawn, you’ll understand this one. It is the most beautiful sight in the world, bar none.
    • Vadre Fjord: Waterford, known as Vadrefjord to its Viking founders, has a Norse history dating back to the ninth century. Vadre Fjord means Water Ford and is where the English name comes from.
  2. In 1793 AD Work commenced on Waterford’s first bridge, dubbed “Timbertoes” by the locals, (now as we all know the name of the main bar in the bridge hotel) because of its wooden construction.
  3. Waterford is the oldest city in Ireland. A walled city of Viking origins, it retains much of it’s medieval character together with the graceful buildings from its 18th century expansion.
  4. Waterford’s Reginald’s Tower is the oldest and most historic urban medieval monument in Ireland.
  5. The well known phrase ‘by hook or by crook’ originated here from the place names Hook and Crooke when Cromwell vowed that his armies would take Waterford ‘by Hook or by Crooke’.
  6. Pay through the nose – The Vikings were so fierce that even the normally lethal native tribe of Waterford (the Déise) were forced into submission and were only too happy to pay Airgead Sroine (nose money) in order to keep their noses on their faces. This Viking de-nasification is the origin of the expression "to pay through the nose!!!""
  7. Waterford is the only city in Europe to have a Roman Catholic and a Protestant Cathedral both designed and built by the same man.
  8. The modern method of Bacon Curing was developed in Waterford.
  9. Waterford was the only city besieged by Cromwell which he failed to capture.
  10. The Roman Catholic Cathedral in Waterford is the oldest in Ireland.
  11. Waterford for centuries was Ireland’s second city.
  12. The only family in the world which has a father and son decorated with the Victoria Cross for bravery is the Roberts family from Waterford City
  13. The first GAA competition was held in Tramore, Co. Waterford
  14. Waterford is the oldest City in Ireland.
  15. The Irish Tri-Colour was created by Thomas Francis Meagher, a Waterford man.
  16. A Waterford man named Luke Wadding set up St. Patrick’s day.
  17. Waterford Glass is the original and best glass in the World.
  18. The Suir is Europe’s fastest flowing River.
  19. Ryan-Air operated in Waterford Airport before becoming what it is today.
  20. Many people in Newfoundland speak with a Waterford accent.
