Retail

There are shopping centres located in different towns and cities throughout the county, and there are many other shops located on streets and in pedestrian squares also.
"Whoever said money can't buy happiness simply didn't know where to shop"
- BO Derek

City Square Shopping Centre

City Square Shopping Centre located in Waterford City is home to more than 30 stores, including a range of women's, men's and children's fashions. The centre's major shops include Debenhams, River Island, Dunnes Stores, Next and Champion Sports. Additionally, there are a number of cafes and restaurants, such as Cafe Kylemore, Shake Shack and The Brasserie.
City Square Shopping Centre is conveniently located near the quays in Waterford City and also offers covered parking for 500 cars.

Dungarvan Shopping Centre

"50 Shops, One Great location" really sums up Dungarvan Shopping Centre, a shopping centre on par with anything the country has to offer.Dungarvan Shopping Centre has established itself as the number one shopping centre in the south east and really has become the centre of Dungarvan.
Brands such as Dunne's Stores, Lloyds Pharmacy, Eason, McDonalds are located here, along with local owned businesses such as Shanique Boutique and Carraig Donn fashions.
With a range of restaurants and coffee shops, we will ensure your visit to Dungarvan Shopping Centre will be a complete shopping experience.

Lisduggan Shopping Centre

Recessions have come and gone but Lisduggan Shopping Centre Waterford continues to thrive and has attracted several new tenants in recent years like Dealz, Clearout, Phelan's Shoes and Travel Creations, which is a testament to the vibrancy of this centre. The centre can boast 100% occupancy of all 22 units at the present time.
The goal of Lisduggan Shopping Centre Waterford is to provide a comprehensive range of shops to meet the daily needs of the local community and to offer better value to their customers than any other location in the Waterford city as well as the benefit of 600 free car parking spaces.
